How matching works

Five ways VybeCheck finds your match.

VybeCheck goes beyond collecting votes. It finds signal in your answers and your X identity. Every match is grounded in a real profile. Anonymity isn't an option, because anonymous noise would make every match meaningless.

01

Match by similar answers

Your answers map to a score vector across humour, spontaneity, values, taste and energy. VybeCheck ranks everyone in the session by closeness and shows why: "you both chose X on 6 of 10 questions."

Match you to a Vybe archetype

Questions map to archetype dimensions. Your answers produce a type like "Calm Chaos," "Soft Speculator," or "Main Character Energy," complete with a breakdown so it feels earned, not random.

Match compatible opposites

Some dimensions are marked complementary, like planner versus spontaneous. VybeCheck scores "fit" using compatibility rules, not just similarity, and shows the logic: "you like structure, they like novelty. Strong fit."

Match you to content & creators

VybeCheck learns what session styles you engage with: serious, flirty, funny, deep. From there it recommends sessions and creators whose formats match your natural response pattern.

Your Vybe trend over time

Your answers build a baseline Vybe profile that nudges slightly with every new session. VybeCheck shows "you today" vs "you last month" and surfaces who's trending closer to your current Vybe, creating a loop that keeps the experience meaningful over time.

FAQ

Plain answers to real questions.

What is a session?

A Vybe session is a question with answer options that you publish and share. Each session generates a unique link, collects real-time votes, and produces a shareable results card when it closes or expires. Free users get 3 sessions per day. Creator and Studio plans get unlimited sessions.

What are Vybes credits?

Vybes credits are optional tokens that unlock premium features including extra sessions beyond your daily limit, advanced result breakdowns, audience filters, and creator templates. They're completely optional. The core voting experience is always free for you and your audience. Credits never expire, so you can stock up and use them whenever you need the extra power.

Do I need an X account to use VybeCheck?

VybeCheck requires signing in with a real X (Twitter) account. Anonymous participation isn't allowed. The entire matching engine runs on real identity: your vote history, your answer patterns, your Vybe profile over time. Without a real account, your responses can't be matched, stored or compared. You'd effectively be a ghost in the system. We use your X account to build your Vybe fingerprint, not to post on your behalf.

Why can't I participate anonymously?

Because anonymity destroys matching accuracy. When people vote with no identity attached, they vote differently — more extreme, less consistent, less honest. The match pool fills with noise instead of signal. VybeCheck's whole value is connecting you with people who genuinely share your opinions, so real identity is the foundation, not a gatekeeping choice. Your X account creates the stake that makes your Vybe score trustworthy.

Can I share my results publicly?

That's the whole point. Every completed session generates a results card that you can share directly on X (or anywhere else). Creator and Studio plans get enhanced export kits with styled visuals. The results card shows vote percentages, the winning option, and the total vote count.

Does buying credits interrupt an active session?

Never. Credits are a separate action from running a session. Voting starts the moment you publish — no payment step in the middle. You can buy credits before, during, or after a session and they'll be waiting in your balance. We designed this specifically so that your social momentum is never broken by a checkout flow.

What platforms are supported at launch?

The initial launch surface is X (Twitter). VybeCheck sessions are shareable on any platform via link, but the native share integration, card preview, and social hooks are optimised for X at launch. Support for Threads, LinkedIn, and more social surfaces are on the roadmap based on early traction.
